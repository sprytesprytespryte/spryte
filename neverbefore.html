NEVER BEFORE

Rashi Rajguru

In April 2019, an international team of scientists released to the public the "first ever image of a black hole". The image was presented via a synchronised unveiling across in-person and livestreamed press conferences hosted in countries and institutions affiliated with the ongoing research project. The image quickly slid through digital news platforms, forums, social media, endlessly mutating along the way - even becoming a viral meme which, with all its variations, reflected an audience response seemingly made up from equal parts of awe and bemusement. The next day, print newspapers across the planet followed suit.

This is a carrier bag of nodes with I collected between 2020 and 2022 and then subjectively plotted to orbit around this black hole image, like some edited star system.
It's a mix of research and materials which, to me, fall within the image's sphere of influence; a mixture of causes, feelings and effects. This research looks at the leaky socio-historical feedback loops which can power an image; cultural anchors within science fiction and expectations on current technological progress; various spectacle- and event-based media which fuel relationships to images; social and technical relationships to vision; the absolute need for funding and its relationship to the major framework of science communication; the taxonomy of big things in deep space...

This upload is a compressed and stripped version of some of the research that I gathered during this time. The full collection was designed, edited and intended for print (something I still very much hope to do), so to ensure its readability online some materials have been omitted.
